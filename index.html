Welcome to Le Page Tennis Club

Our Club and Mission

Welcome to Our Premier Tennis Club: Where World-Class Tennis Experiences and a Vibrant, Inclusive Community Converge to Fulfill Our Club's Vision and Mission. Nestled within our Beautiful Grounds, we proudly boast six meticulously maintained en-tout-cas (clay) courts, three of which are Floodlit for extended play, providing the perfect surface for players to refine their skills, engage in thrilling matches, and experience the unique challenges and rewards of clay court tennis. With state-of-the-art facilities, professional coaching, and diverse programs, we are dedicated to nurturing a love for the game, instilling core values, and empowering individuals to achieve their goals. At our club, whether you're a seasoned player or a beginner, you will find a welcoming and supportive environment where you can elevate your game and connect with fellow tennis enthusiasts. Join us and indulge in the pleasure of playing on our exceptional clay courts, surrounded by a vibrant tennis community. Together, we strive to create lasting memories, inspire tennis excellence, and build a legacy of greatness, embracing the thrill of the game every step of the way.

At our tennis club, our vision is to foster a vibrant community that celebrates the joy of tennis and promotes an inclusive and supportive environment. We strive to create a space where players of all ages and skill levels can come together, connect, and excel in the sport they love. Through top-notch facilities, expert coaching, and a commitment to sportsmanship, we aim to inspire and empower our members to reach their full potential, both on and off the court

Keen to establish a local tennis complex, interested citizens met in 1966 to form a general committee. Le Page Tennis Club was founded, with the club’s inaugural meeting held in March of that same year. The initial proposed plan was for five porous courts and a club house. A co-operative was formed to raise the funds required for the project, and the process was underway. A seven year lease was attained from the council for use of the land. Three courts were opened for play in January 1968, with competitive matches beginning from April of that year. The club house was likewise completed in April and its first meeting held in that month. Courts 4 and 5 were completed in 1970, with the addition of floodlights in 1973 allowing for night matches. The grounds took on their current layout when court 6 was built in 1975.
